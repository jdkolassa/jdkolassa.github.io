&#8220;She&#8217;s a looker,&#8221; Lukas said, casually glancing in her direction. There was no doubt she was pretty, even under that makeup intended to evoke memories of ancient Egyptia, but Lukas had seen a lot of lookers in his century spanning career. Just because they had looks didn&#8217;t mean their clients would be happy.

Martelli smirked. Guy was as dumb as a lunar puppy, and about as pretty as one too. &#8220;Straight from Alpha Centauri, I kid you not.&#8221; (_No, you never would_.) &#8220;Would you believe that? The Old Core.&#8221; He said the last three words with an almost mystical reverence, slowly, light at first, building up to the end. Lukas suppressed a snort. No good to hurt the poor man&#8217;s feelings.

&#8220;Why she wearing a space suit?&#8221; he asked, then added, &#8220;And an ancient one, at that? Go along with the makeup or something? Got an old vibe going on?&#8221;

For what it was worth, the woman just sat on the bed, looking between them, when she wasn&#8217;t staring at the ceiling, acted bored in their presence. Lukas found it almost charming.

As opposed to Martelli&#8217;s glare. &#8220;The Old Core,&#8221; he intoned. &#8220;Birthplace of humanity. Everything is old there.&#8221;

Lukas nodded. Point. &#8220;Well, then, does she have anything other than looks?&#8221;

Martelli, still scowling a little bit, turned to her. &#8220;Please, play that song. The one you played to me earlier.&#8221;

The woman nodded gracefully, slowly got off the bed, and stood&#8211;and that&#8217;s when the assault began.

For the next four minutes, Lukas&#8217; ears were battered and beaten by an onslaught of sound. The woman&#8217;s voice skyrocketed to the stars and swung low to the depths of the largest gas giant. There was no roughness, no crack, no sign that she was inadequate at her job; her singing was so smooth that no imperfection could ever be gleaned; if she was a sphere, then she was created in the complete absence of gravity. Yet it was so intense, so _visceral_, that Lukas was stunned, literally and figuratively.

And that was just the auditory portion.

The woman would not stand still for a nanosecond. She thrusted her arms into the air and pulled them back, she stuck out her hips and gyrated around, and she even caressed Lukas&#8217; mug, which for just a moment broke the spell as he felt the artificial synthskin of the suit&#8217;s gloves. But the moment she left him, he was firmly entrapped again.

And when it was all over, she stopped, sat down on the bed, and once again looked disinterested.

It took a moment for Lukas&#8217; body to register it was over. And when it did, all he said was, &#8220;Wow.&#8221;

&#8220;The Old Core.&#8221;

For once, Lukas had to agree. &#8220;Just incredible. File?&#8221;

Martelli handed over the flat datapad. One of Lukas&#8217; first rules was never to use a transmission, even at short range, that could be intercepted. He glanced over the file, saw everything was in order, but blinked at the origin point. It was just two letters, repeated. GA GA. Some sort of code? He pointed to it so Martelli could see.

The other man shrugged. &#8220;No idea.&#8221;

&#8220;It&#8217;ll be no good if this is based on some sort of robot,&#8221; Lukas&#8217; snapped, though even as he said it, he admitted it did have some sort of novelty.

&#8220;No, no no!&#8221; Martelli said. &#8220;No, it&#8217;s real! It&#8217;s based on a real person, trust me!&#8221; The man licked his lips. &#8220;You know, Ancient Earth! That was thousands of years ago! Very strange naming conventions!&#8221;

Lukas blinked, and that wasn&#8217;t something he ever felt doing often. &#8220;Are you seriously trying to peddle that she&#8217;s based on an _Earthian_?&#8221;

Martelli nodded. He might be stupid, but he wouldn&#8217;t lie like that. He was far too afraid to. Lukas rubbed his chin. If it were true&#8230;if they had an Earthian based&#8230;man. They&#8217;d be the richest fools in the entire Confederation. And Alpha Centauri _was_ the closest colony world to Ancient Earth, that much was certain.

&#8220;Alright,&#8221; Lukas said, turning towards the door. &#8220;This is going to be a limited edition model. Not just anybody can have it. Get your contact on Alpha and tell him we&#8217;ll order a hundred thousand&#8211;no, make that fifty thousand copies. I&#8217;ll find the wealthiest sons of patooches out there who will want to drop a few million on a simmbot based on an Ancient Earthian singer.&#8221; He stopped, and frowned. &#8220;And get her out of that spacesuit. She&#8217;s clearly not supposed to be in it.&#8221;

The gynoid silently complied, even as Martelli said, &#8220;She was programmed with piloting skills. Her designers say she&#8217;s cleared to fly any yacht up to a Class C heavy.&#8221;

Lukas smiled. &#8220;Can she, now? Well, then, their price&#8211;and our profits&#8211;just doubled.&#8221;
