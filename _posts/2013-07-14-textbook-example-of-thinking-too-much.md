# The Pixar Theory: Every Character Lives in the Same Universe

> _Jon Negroni spent one year untangling the secret world hidden deep within Pixar films. This thesis (printed in full below) originally appeared on <a href="http://jonnegroni.com/2013/07/11/the-pixar-theory/" target="_blank">his personal blog</a> and quickly became a viral sensation. Negroni continues to update his post based on interesting feedback from readers._
> 
> Several months ago, I watched a <a href="http://www.cracked.com/video_18459_why-pixar-movies-are-all-secretly-about-apocalypse.html" target="_blank">fun-filled video on Cracked.com</a> that introduced the idea (at least to me) that all of the Pixar movies actually exist within the same universe.
> 
> Since then, I’ve obsessed over this concept, working to complete what I call “The Pixar Theory,” a working narrative that ties all of the Pixar movies into one cohesive timeline with a main theme.
> 
> This theory covers every Pixar production since _Toy Story_: _A Bug’s Life_, _Toy Story 2_, _Monsters Inc._, _Finding Nemo_, _The Incredibles_, _Cars_, _Ratatouille_, _Wall-E_, _Up_, _Toy Story 3_, _Cars 2_, _Brave _and _Monsters University_.
