[Anonymous Contributions: Ghostwriters Help Less Skilled Writers Complete Books | Getting Ahead | Express Night Out](http://www.expressnightout.com/content/2011/04/anonymous-contributions-ghostwriters-getting-ahead.php).

Personally, I would never be a ghostwriter. The main job of a ghostwriter is not to write one&#8217;s own story, but the story of someone else, in _their_ voice. (The chief difference in work, therefore, between ghostwriters and biographers.) Plus you don&#8217;t get your name on the cover, and let&#8217;s face it, that&#8217;s a huge ego boost to any writer.

But I don&#8217;t diss the profession. There are a lot of people out there that have a message or tale and just aren&#8217;t very good at writing it. There is nothing wrong with helping someone else do that. That&#8217;s just not _me_. I have my own things to write. Still, an interesting look into one writing profession.

I think the best thing I found in this article was the Bethesda Writer&#8217;s Center, which is not that far away from me. They have a _ton_ of interesting classes, including&#8211;and I&#8217;m not kidding here&#8211;a class on turning your short story into a novel. Now wouldn&#8217;t _that_ be neat! That&#8217;s something I&#8217;ve thought about the story I&#8217;m working on now; I actually wanted to write a novel, but I figured I should write it in short story form first.

If I can fit it in my budget, I think I may take that class. Then I could be the next [Jack Vance](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Dying_Earth) and have a [system of magic named after me](http://tvtropes.org/pmwiki/pmwiki.php/Main/VancianMagic).
