> It’s fair to say that Rep. Emanuel Cleaver has a way with words. The Congressional Black Caucus chairman — who famously described last August’s debt deal as a “sugar-coated Satan sandwich” — now has a fan club of members from across the political spectrum for eccentric weekly letters he pens on congressional civility&#8230;.

via [Dear colleague: Let’s talk vampires, cooking frogs &#8211; Kate Nocera &#8211; POLITICO.com](http://www.politico.com/news/stories/0712/78870_Page2.html).

Let&#8217;s go to the second page of this story, where we find this:

> Though he’s been in Congress since 2005, Cleaver says the discourse from both Democrats and Republicans on TV and on the floor has gotten considerably worse. He started writing the letters after reaching a tipping point last year. There was no single incident that prompted him but, rather, it was a “preponderance of incidents and television acts of incivility and legislative inertia.” He’s not singling out any political party — he said he is a fan of healthy partisanship — but he has trouble with the way people talk about each other.
> 
> “I disagree with Paul Ryan — the budget that came out of the committee he chairs is not one that I could embrace. **But I will not refer to it as the ‘Ryan budget,’ because that’s pejorative,” he said.** “Some of my own folks said, ‘We’ve got to say that because we need people to know how bad it is.’ And I said, ‘Do you think you can work with this man if you go around talking that?’ You can say you disagree with him, but you don’t have to make him into Satan Jr. to disagree.”

Emphasis mine.

I don&#8217;t understand this at all. How is it pejorative? The budget plan was produced by Paul Ryan, so it would be natural to refer to it as the Ryan Budget, as to differentiate it from the Amash Budget or the Pelosi Budget. (Oh, who am I kidding, Pelosi has never made a budget.) This is the same kind of silliness that says the &#8220;Democrat Party&#8221; is a pejorative, rather than just a stupid error, and that we must not call PPACA &#8220;Obamacare&#8221; because that is pejorative, even though it was championed and more or less created by Obama and his staff. (I got a _lot_ of liberals who were very, very angry with me for calling it Obamacare. It still makes no sense.)

Look, the guy came up with it. It is not &#8220;pejorative&#8221; to attach it to his name. It&#8217;s simply descriptive.

This is yet another example of Washington Orwellianism at work. It&#8217;s the same logic that has a decrease in the rate of spending increases labeled a &#8220;cut,&#8221; [even though spending in the next cycle will still be higher in the previous cycle](http://danieljmitchell.wordpress.com/2010/12/01/fiscal-commission-is-using-washingtons-dishonest-budget-math/). As a writer who believes in using clear English*, this drives me insane. It has to stop.

<span style="font-size: xx-small;">*Though I accept that language is fluid and changes constantly, there are certain lines which, if crossed, render everything meaningless. This is one of them.</span>
