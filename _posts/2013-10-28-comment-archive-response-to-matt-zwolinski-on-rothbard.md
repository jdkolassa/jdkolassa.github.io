So to preserve my comment in case of an error, here is my comment <a href="http://bleedingheartlibertarians.com/2013/10/seven-cheers-for-murray-rothbard" target="_blank">on Matt Zwolinski&#8217;s blog post on Murray Rothbard</a>:

<div dir="auto" data-role="message">
  <blockquote>
    <p>
      Hmm.
    </p>
    
    <p>
      Inasmuch as Rothbard actually made people question the state itself, and thus give them the alternative framework to &#8220;the state must do everything,&#8221; I can give him credit. But I think in the end Rothbard&#8217;s anarcho-capitalism may have damaged the liberty movement more than it helped. One of the reasons people don&#8217;t take libertarianism seriously is because we have a ton of people running around saying we should just abolish government entirely. People don&#8217;t generally take those radical approaches that easily, not unless there is mass starvation and violence going on.
    </p>
    
    <p>
      Also, the paleo strategy was really, really bad. That alone should make one question Rothbard&#8217;s judgement, if he was a &#8220;happy warrior,&#8221; and if someone would want to hang out with him. By all accounts he was kind of nutty, and if he&#8217;s writing newsletters blaming blacks for all of society&#8217;s ills, then he&#8217;s kind of not a charming and delightful person, but just a racist with a veneer of geniality.
    </p>
    
    <p>
      I agree with Brennan. Rothbard is a hack, and his disciple Rockwell (and that other guy, Hoppe) has continued tainting libertarianism with some pretty despicable ideas. I&#8217;m not sure I would give him three cheers, let alone seven. Maybe one. And it would be lukewarm.
    </p>
    
    <p>
      I will agree with you on that there shouldn&#8217;t be a war between BHL and LvMI types. Except for when the LvMI types express some abhorrent views on race and sexuality, but other than that, you are correct. There is a lot of common ground. (Although praxeology befuddles me a bit&#8230;)
    </p>
