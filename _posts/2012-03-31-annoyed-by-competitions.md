So the D.C. Shorts screenplay competition page says the following:

> Want to save money on the entry fees?  Enter early to pay the lower fee — then send the materials as late as the material deadline below.

Yet, when you try to do that, there is no option to do so&#8211;their submission software, &#8220;Withoutabox,&#8221; forces you to upload your script immediately, even if its not ready.

It doesn&#8217;t seem like there&#8217;s an ability to contact them about this either; they just say if you ask them about submission issues they&#8217;ll send you a form letter.

Utterly bizarre. Part of it is my fault&#8211;I should have been ready earlier&#8211;but I was figuring that I could just send in the online application and pay now, then send the script later, thus save about $30. Not so, which makes me think there&#8217;s something sleazy about it. I&#8217;m sure that&#8217;s just my inner paranoid, but still, it bothers me.

Of course I will still submit, though. It&#8217;s just I&#8217;m going to be paying a bit more.
