[27 Science Fictions That Became Science Facts In 2012](http://www.buzzfeed.com/donnad/27-science-fictions-that-became-science-facts-in-2).

This is truly a great list here from the folks at BuzzFeed. The past year&#8211;indeed, the past decade&#8211;have seen some amazing technological leaps and discoveries. The science fiction world of even the 1980s, or even the mid 1990s, is within our grasp. And I suspect that by 2022, only ten years from now, ideas that we have floating around now in our heads will be within our grasp too.

The only thing I quibble with is 18. No doubt that&#8217;s cool. But are solar panels really reaching the point where they&#8217;re competitive with fossil fuels? No. Not by a long shot. I don&#8217;t care if that thing is half the price of traditional solar panels. You need more than just the input cost to make that kind of evaluation. What kind of energy levels do you get out of it? Is it anywhere near enough energy to supply the world? I don&#8217;t think so. Robert Bryce in the _Wall Street Journal_ notes [that demand for electricity increases so fast we would have to add one Brazil worth of power generators to the energy sector every year.](http://online.wsj.com/article_email/SB10001424127887324640104578161593492943144-lMyQjAxMTAyMDEwNzExNDcyWj.html?mod=wsj_valettop_email) You can&#8217;t do that with solar panels; they just don&#8217;t generate enough.

Also, 13, 19, and 26: they&#8217;re cool and all, but is that really the &#8220;future&#8221;? Hardly. They are discoveries out there, but the &#8220;future&#8221; is really predicated on technology. That stuff is just seeing things from very far away and in the past. Even though 19 is _really_ cool.

Otherwise, though, a stupendous list. I look forward to the next article BuzzFeed writes in 2013.

You may ask why I put down &#8220;Part 1&#8221; in the title. That&#8217;s because I was originally planning on doing a &#8220;Welcome to the Future&#8221; post in January, after the apocalypse ended, because in 2013, that&#8217;s when the notion of the &#8220;future&#8221; in terms of science fiction really hits home. But I&#8217;ll save that for January.

Until then, welcome to the future. It&#8217;s already here. And it&#8217;s getting better all the time.
