(Lately, that&#8217;s mostly turned into &#8220;Reread all of _The Dresden Files_ and play _Geneforge_,&#8221; the latter of which I will rant about later, but, you know.) My favorite fiction show on TV right now is, without a doubt, Fox&#8217;s _Fringe_. It&#8217;s got all the bases covered: plausible science, awesome science fiction, alternate universe, great characters, humor, and wonderfully fantastic soundtracks. Lately, though, I&#8217;ve noticed that the _Fringe_ airing nowadays is not the same _Fringe_ that started airing four seasons ago. (Oddly enough, this is quite similar to the show&#8217;s current concept, where there are different universes that are mostly, but not _quite_ the same.) The original _Fringe_ was something like an updated _X-Files_ for the 21st century, though with JJ Abrams at the helm, it definitely had a _real_ mythology behind it, much more concrete, but still, it was them going out and chasing down monsters and investigating weird anomalies that turned out to be &#8220;scientific crimes&#8221; of one sort or another. But then it slowly morphed into this large battle between two parallel universes, which have now come to some uneasy sort of truce, but there&#8217;s still reality degradation going on that they both have to stop. I&#8217;m not saying it&#8217;s bad, mind you, I&#8217;m just saying it&#8217;s different. I can&#8217;t really put my finger on it, but the character interactions have changed, the aims have changed, the entire _tone_ of the show has changed. Most of it&#8217;s for the better, but some of it is just&#8230;different.

[Which is why the following tidbit](http://www.tvline.com/2011/11/fringe-joshua-jackson-preview-season-4-episode-6/), which I found through a random search on reviews of Friday&#8217;s episode, is pants-crappingly awesome:
