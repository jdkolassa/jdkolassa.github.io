[Idiot anti-smoking advocates are upset about &#8216;Rango.&#8217;](http://filmdrunk.uproxx.com/2011/03/thats-it-everyones-fired).

Far better blog post on what the heck is wrong with all those dopes who got into a hissy fit over _Rango_. Certainly better than this [weaksauce post](http://reason.com/blog/2011/03/14/a-lot-of-kids-are-going-to-sta) by the otherwise extremely strong and intelligent _Reason_ magazine, by far one of the best mags out there. Their post only says, more or less, &#8220;you have to think of context when the character is smoking.&#8221; They could have gone a lot farther. Like Mr. Filmdrunk, up there. (Bet he won&#8217;t be winning accolades from anybody in the no-smoking crowd.)

(I&#8217;m actually a little surprised at how weak the _Reason_ one is. I really expected them to drop a sledgehammer on these guys. Oh well.)

The best comment about this all, though, comes from a comment on the Filmdrunk site:

> <span style="font-size: 14px; font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; line-height: normal;">Seriously. You wonder why films have become so commercialized – it’s because of groups like these who actually think movies are commercials instead of stories with characters who sometimes do naughty things.</span>

And that pretty much sums up my disgust with these groups from a storyteller&#8217;s perspective.
