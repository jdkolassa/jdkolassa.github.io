A not so long time ago, in a boardroom not all _that_ far away&#8230;

So the news is out. The Star Wars Expanded Universe &#8211; all the books, games, comics, etc beyond the movies and the _Clone Wars_ show &#8211; <a href="http://starwars.com/news/the-legendary-star-wars-expanded-universe-turns-a-new-page.html" target="_blank">is essentially being put into storage</a>. It will still be available, printed, and even to be used by authors and creators in the &#8220;new&#8221; timeline. Already, <a href="http://www.reddit.com/r/StarWars/comments/243c13/why_the_expanded_universes_death_hurts/" target="_blank">some fans are complaining hard about this</a>, feeling they&#8217;re being Force choked by this decision. Things like &#8220;I invested years and lots of money into this!&#8221; are getting bandied about.

These complaints are really childish, and I actually think this is a good decision for Disney to make.

First, why the decision is good. While I certainly have my favorites from the EU canon &#8211; the _X-Wing _series, _Young Jedi Knights_, anything by Timothy Zahn, and _I, Jedi_ all stand out &#8211; we must face the fact that a great majority of the Expanded Universe is, to put it frankly, bantha poodoo. <a href="http://jdkolassa.net/2014/01/i-am-not-a-committee-disney-to-form-group-to-kill-star-wars-expanded-universe/" target="_blank">I said as much back in January</a>, and I just want to reiterate it more now. _The Courtship of Princess Leia_ is as schlocky as they come, and _The Crystal Star_ is&#8230;well, perhaps the less said about it, the better. There are many horrible <a href="http://jdkolassa.net/2011/01/star-wrongs/" target="_blank">Star Wrongs</a> in the EU. In no way, shape, or form should J.J Abrams and the crew working on the next trilogy be beholden to them. I&#8217;m glad they won&#8217;t be!

But then, that&#8217;s the thing, isn&#8217;t it? The EU was _always_ secondary canon. Even though it was canon &#8211; unlike, say, Star Trek, which basically said that it&#8217;s &#8220;extended universe&#8221; was _not_ canon, no way, no how &#8211; it was always secondary to the two film trilogies and later on _Clone Wars_. (Ugh.) So even they technically were not really _Star Wars_. And for the vast majority of people who have watched the movies, they aren&#8217;t, _because they&#8217;re not the movies_. How many people read _Star Wars_ novels? I&#8217;m sure it&#8217;s a large number, but it is nowhere near as large as the many who have watched the movies and never delved into the EU.

Now, why the complaints, in most cases, are pretty childish: because this decision in no way takes away from your enjoyment of those stories. We are dealing with a fictional universe, and you know where fictional universes live? In your mind, and in your heart. And nothing that Disney can do can take them away from those places. Sure, they may not exist in the same timeline as the new movies&#8230;_however_, fiction loves alternate timelines, so there is nothing saying you can&#8217;t just push the old EU to a different timeline and enjoy that. People are doing it with Star Trek, why not Star Wars?

Fact is, the _Star Wars_ universe is one you make of it. I myself have completely disavowed anything from _New Jedi Order_ onwards and most of the stuff set between the Exar Kun saga and the earliest &#8220;Last Days of the Old Republic&#8221; type material. (KOTOR, KOTOR II, _The Old Republic_, etc &#8211; all that stuff doesn&#8217;t exist for me, because it is all hilariously dumb.) Does that mean that content doesn&#8217;t exist for other fans? Of course not.

I really think fans need to get over themselves. Saying &#8220;the death of the EU hurts&#8221; is almost pathetic. Having your dog die hurts. Having a friend or family member hurts. Losing a job hurts. A company making a change in what novels it will accept as a backstory to a movie series doesn&#8217;t hurt, and if does you may be overly attached. Sorry if that sounds harsh, but come on. I love fiction as much as the next guy, and I love writing it and yes I do get attached to things sometimes, but at the end of the day these are all stories that exist _in your mind_. (Well, until Heinlein&#8217;s pantheistic solipsism is proven correct, that is&#8230;)

It&#8217;s whiny, entitled bullshit. I see this all over the &#8216;Net on all sorts of topics, and when it comes to something as silly as this &#8211; yes, &#8220;silly&#8221; and _Star Wars_ **do** go together in this case &#8211; it just makes me sad. Sad that people are wasting their time on this. Move on, you have more important things to do. The Expanded Universe you love will always be there for you, forever.

(And before you say &#8220;But Jeremy &#8211; shouldn&#8217;t you have more important things to do than write a blog post like this?&#8221; why yes, yes I do. Which is watch the rest of _Gundam Unicorn_.)
