There&#8217;s been a lot of talk of taxes lately. Namely, Obama is raising taxes in his new budget&#8211;[which will go nowhere](http://www.unitedliberty.org/articles/9581-obama-rolls-out-another-budget-the-senate-wont-pass)&#8211;and there are still people talking about flat taxes and fair taxes. So here&#8217;s my idea.

  1. Individual income tax 
      1. 12% on all incomes up to $500,000
      2. 20% on all incomes past $500,000
      3. Eliminate all deductions and credits; keep only a $10,000 standard deduction, a 1-to-1 charitable deduction, and 1-to-1 tax credits for spending on private education and healthcare costs.
  2. Corporate income tax: 20%
  3. Pigovian taxes on various items (booze, cigarettes, marijuana&#8211;which would be legalized, obviously&#8211;and perhaps pornography.)

Is this a perfectly libertarian tax system? Of course not, but it&#8217;s far and away better than what we have today. It would eliminate nearly all of the confusion and social engineering that goes on with today&#8217;s tax code, while drastically cutting tax rates yet establishing a far fairer system, one into which everyone pays. At the same time, it makes it easier for the poor with a generous standard deduction, encourages charitable donations (which, I&#8217;ll admit, is a piece of social engineering) and also makes education and health care tax free, which saves people a bundle while dramatically improving their lives. It also has something of a progressive tax rate that will be red meat for liberals and progressives.

Naturally, I would want a flat tax on consumption&#8211;something like 10%&#8211;with exemptions for food (&#8216;cept caviar), clothing (&#8216;cept Prada), housing (&#8216;cept 50-room mansions), medicine (&#8216;cept plastic surgery), and education (&#8216;cept&#8230;well, nothing, in this case) but until we get the 16th Amendment repealed, it would just add another tax on everything else, rather than actually replacing the system entirely.

Of course, with either system, would come a dramatic cut in spending. Some agencies and departments I would cut include, but are not limited to:

  * Department of Homeland Security
  * TSA
  * FCC
  * FTC
  * ATF
  * DEA
  * Commerce Department
  * Housing and Urban Development
  * Energy Department
  * Department of Agriculture
  * Combine Labor, Education, and Health into one department
  * Cut military spending by 50% and end all overseas bases
  * Turn Medicare into block grants
  * Force Social Security to undergo massive reform, including raising ages
  * Eliminate all welfare programs and replace them with one guaranteed minimum wage, to be contingent on reintegration trajectories and means-testing, and primarily focusing on vocational education

Yeah. Reasonable tax system, dramatically lower spending, better system overall. I&#8217;ll be blunt: I really don&#8217;t get why people can&#8217;t get this.
