I haven&#8217;t read _The Hunger Games_, an oversight on my part, apparently. People are telling me I should read the books and watch the movie, because they are really really really really really really really really good. But I was watching  _Good Morning America_ while waiting for my bus, and they were discussing how many fans were going to the forest in North Carolina where they shot the film and looking for stuff, or reenacting the scenes.

And then there was the obligatory opening night shot with the screaming fans. I mean, _howling_. Utterly insane. What drives people to that? Are their lives so pale, so lacking in substance, they turn into a pack of wolves for something fictional? 

Don&#8217;t get me wrong. I love fiction. I write it. I enjoy it. There are some fandoms I&#8217;m nuts about&#8211;_Star Wars_, _The Dresden Files_, among others&#8211;and some people, my parents especially, think my head is in the clouds. But I&#8217;m nowhere near as crazy as this. 

I wonder what it says about society. Not too long ago, there was a scientist who conjectured that we wouldn&#8217;t meet aliens, because instead of going out and travelling among the stars, they would becombecome narcissist and turn inwards, being absorbed by their equivalent of pop culture. As with all conjecture about extraterrestrial societies, its really about our own.

I would like having fans. I would like to have a very popular novel series that turns into a movie franchise. But good lord, I don&#8217;t think I want my fans to be that crazy.
