About a week ago, I was at my sister&#8217;s house in upstate New York visiting, and she told me about this wonderful thing called X10. It&#8217;s a company that develops &#8220;home automation systems,&#8221; allowing you to control your lighting, doors, appliances, and everything remotely, or set up schedules for turning things on or off or doing other crazy things. Her example was using a TV remote to control the lighting in her home theater, which, actually, _was_ really neat. I mean, she had those light cables they string down the aisles at the movie theater, and she could dim them so it looked like Regal Cinemas&#8230;you just have to trust me on this one. It was cool.

But then she showed me their website, because, well, [it&#8217;s not what you expect](http://www.x10.com/homepage.htm).

For a contrast, let&#8217;s take a brief look at the [Apple website](http://www.apple.com/mac/). (I&#8217;ll move past the special splash page about Steve Jobs&#8211;not to belittle him, but simply because that&#8217;s not the regular design.) Slick, eh? It&#8217;s pretty, clean, and, if you&#8217;re a _Firefly_ fan like me, deserving of the descriptor &#8220;shiny.&#8221; It&#8217;s like everything else with Apple&#8211;very pretty packaging.

Now back to X10. Now back to Apple. Back to X10. I think you can see the difference.

My sister said she thought that X10 was originally scam of some sort, with all of this &#8220;Buy this! Sale now! Speaker Palooza!&#8221; malarkey. Yet, it apparently worked, since my sister and her husband have already bought some of their products and, I think, they&#8217;re looking to buy some more. Another thing they told me is that you won&#8217;t see their ads on TV, or around the Internet, either&#8211;I&#8217;m not exactly sure how they found out about it, but it looks like our winner is &#8220;word of mouth&#8221; (followed by a _ding! ding! ding!_ noise.) Of course, I&#8217;m helping it along too. I know that. Really.

So, basically, these guys have a really cool product, which they are apparently making a good deal of money off of (enough to sell their own $200 Android tablet, which I&#8217;m sure would also have their apps for controlling your garage door from work), and they don&#8217;t spend a great deal of money on either web design or advertising. I would really like to see their profit margin&#8211;I don&#8217;t do stocks, but if I did, I might invest in these guys. Oh, and better still, [they offer a way to kill their ads if they appear on your machine](http://www.x10.com/x10ads.htm). A company offering to take out their own ads for you? Holy moly! Finally, someone who understands the average American! (Yes, I&#8217;m sure there&#8217;s a catch of some sort, but still, the intention is quite noble and refreshing.)

And here I was, thinking we needed all that extra stuff, needed to take extra special time to think about how I would write something or design the presentation. Apparently, not so much.

Personally, if I got a house, I would get [their intercom system](http://www.x10.com/audio_distribution/intercom_system.html?TWENTY11) and make the kids call me Captain Kirk:

> **Child:** Bridge to Captain Kirk.
> 
> **Me:** Kirk here.
> 
> **Child:** Captain, we&#8217;ve just run out of toilet paper, and sensors have detected more Klingons coming from Uranus.
> 
> **Me:** Go to Red Alert and take evasive action. I&#8217;m on my way.

God, I would be so annoying.
