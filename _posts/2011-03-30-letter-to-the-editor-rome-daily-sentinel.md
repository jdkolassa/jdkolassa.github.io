[RomeSentinel.com Letter to the Editor: &#8220;Teachers are Dedicated Professionals.&#8221;](http://romesentinel.com/news?newsid=20110317-142112)

I&#8217;m still working on getting my story typed up and worked on, though I&#8217;m quite busy at work. In the interest of providing fresh content, let me share with you a letter to the editor I wrote to my local paper, which is a response to the letter linked above. It wasn&#8217;t published (as far as I know of), so I&#8217;ll just post it here. It does violate that rule of &#8220;keep it to 150 words,&#8221; but that&#8217;s because the paper in question regularly prints letters twice that size, so the rules are slightly different.

And suffice to say, we two writers have considerably different views on public educators.

My letter:

> Mr. Paul Fitzpatrick wrote in a letter on March 17th that teachers are &#8220;extremely hard working and dedicated professionals&#8221; who work in an &#8220;honorable profession&#8221; and were unfairly denigrated by this paper in an editorial.
> 
> As a person who graduated from RFA*, received my bachelor’s degree, and now work in the nation’s capitol (where I can only periodically read this paper online), allow me to offer my experience. I had a math teacher told us while we were studying for the Regents that we were &#8220;failures&#8221; and should &#8220;drop out,&#8221; that we were worthless. I had another math teacher, when I asked her to explain a concept I did not understand, state that she had to &#8220;move on,&#8221; and never explained the topic. A home-ec teacher told me to &#8220;shut up and do your work&#8221; when I was trying to ask a question clarifying that work. And when bullies stole my stuff or continually harassed me, many teachers just told me to “talk it out”—apparently forgetting that bullies spoke with their fists
> 
> Not all teachers are bad. Many did an admirable job cramming twelve months of material into only eight and still having us understand it all. I respect them. However, there are more than enough who have cast doubt on it being an &#8220;honorable profession.&#8221;
> 
> Jeremy Kolassa, Washington DC

*My old high school. Long may it decay.
