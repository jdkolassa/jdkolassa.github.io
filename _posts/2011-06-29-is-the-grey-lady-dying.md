I follow Romenesko on Twitter. Its the blog at the Poynter Institute that is essentially the water cooler for professional journalists, who got what job, new tips and tricks on how to write stories, &#8220;Oh no they didn&#8217;t!&#8221; That sort of thing. Recently, their tweets have constantly been about people leaving the New York Times&#8211;I mean, _constantly_. [_New York Magazine_ has more:](http://nymag.com/daily/intel/2011/06/jill_abramsons_first_task_stop.html) 

> The departures follow a string of surprising defections that have buffeted the _Times_ in recent months. Over the past year, the paper has lost &#8220;Sunday Business&#8221; editor Tim O&#8217;Brien to the Huffington Post along with fellow business reporter Peter Goodman, Dexter Filkins to the _New Yorker_, Miguel Helft to _Fortune_, and Frank Rich to this magazine, among others. The moves, taken together, represent a rare exodus at the _Times_, historically a magnet for ambitious talent.

In addition to those, David Rohde&#8211;who was captured in Pakistan&#8211;and _Times Magazine_ editor Sue Suellentrop are also leaving. (I felt quoting that much from the rather short blog post was, well, a bit overkill. But you need blockquotes&#8230;)

I really don&#8217;t want to say it, both because its a trite phrase and I&#8217;m not sure if its at all correct, but still, it seems like the rats are fleeing the sinking ship. The Grey Lady, formally an indomitable juggernaut of American journalism, has not been faring well in the 21st century. It has been sluggish&#8211;like many papers&#8211;in responding to the challenges of new media. Two years ago [they had $1 billion in debt](http://www.businessinsider.com/nyt-bosses-explain-1-billion-debt-to-staffers-memo-2009-7) and were trying desperately to avoid bankruptcy. They succeeded, but for how long?

There&#8217;s two reasons, I think, why these things are happening. One is what I just mentioned: dealing with new media. The rapid development of the internet and the blogosphere has taken away the _Times&#8217;_ and other news organizations&#8217; traditional role of being the gatekeeper of information. Former consumers now have alternatives where there previously were none, most of them free. (And light. Have you picked up a copy of the _Times_ lately? My god, now I know how to get my new abs.) It&#8217;s simple [creative destruction](http://www.econlib.org/library/Enc/CreativeDestruction.html), and there&#8217;s nothing wrong with that. The other reason, though, is more important. Namely, the _Times_ is woefully out of touch with America.

Now, this probably isn&#8217;t a shock to you. You&#8217;re probably thinking right now, &#8220;Dude, you&#8217;re preaching to the choir. None of this is news. What is your angle?&#8221; My angle, if there is one, is I that I&#8217;m wondering if the liberal pundits and journalistas at the _Times_ subconsciously agree with us, and are fleeing both to escape the crater that will inevitably bloom and also to save some shred of their liberal world within themselves.

Paul Krugman struts around like a peacock, shilling for the Democratic Party and continually preaching &#8220;Stimulus!&#8221; without having a clue how it really affects the economy. Thomas Friedman (as you no doubt are aware) writes a column based on pure silliness. And the _Times_&#8216; editorial position consistently takes a hard-left tack, from &#8220;free speech for me but not for thee&#8221; on _Citizens United_ and other issues, dismissing the Tea Party movement as the province of &#8220;old rich white people,&#8221; and pressing for additional government expenditures at a time when the government is fresh out of moolah. While that shouldn&#8217;t affect their news side of things, it has made some people question its journalistic credibility as well&#8211;what are they leaving out? How are they skewing things? What are they burying? (And yes, they have buried stories, particularly around the healthcare debate when they ignored New York&#8217;s own stunning health system problems brought about by mandates and similar mechanisms to the federal bill, until just after the bill passed.)

The problem for the _Times&#8217;_ is that the readership who supports this stuff is shrinking, growing increasingly wealthy and out of tune themselves. More and more Americans are questioning the paradigm of what the _Times_ relies on, and the paper blithely ignores this&#8211;to their own peril. And now, per my idle speculation, I&#8217;m beginning to wonder if even liberal writers see the writing on the wall. Maybe they&#8217;re beginning to understand&#8211;if only subconsciously&#8211;that their paradigm is unworkable and ultimately doomed to failure. Perhaps they don&#8217;t want to be at the epicenter when it finally, brutally rears it head.

In any case, I don&#8217;t really want the Grey Lady to pass on. I really like its typesetting. And I need to work on my abs.
