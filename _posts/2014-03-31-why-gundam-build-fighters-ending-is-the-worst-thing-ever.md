<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">I&#8217;ll just say it: I&#8217;m an anime fan. I&#8217;m probably not an otaku – I don&#8217;t worship it that obsessively – but I do love Japanese animation. One of the first cartoons I saw as a child was the original </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Digimon</i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">, and from there I went into the Japanese </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Transformers</i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">, </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Neon Genesis Evangelion</i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">, and all sorts of things. Recently, I&#8217;ve been watching the </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Gundam</i></span> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">franchise, and finding out the latest series will have only one more episode has really made me have some profound thoughts.</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">Yes, </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Gundam Build Fighters</i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">, the dorkiest, lightest, and just utterly most </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>un-Gundam of Gundam shows</i></span> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">has made me have deep thoughts.</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">It&#8217;s okay if you think I&#8217;m crazy. I probably am.</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Gundam Build Fighters</i></span> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">is, from the surface, aimed at children. Even with the battles and the skullduggery that goes on in the background, it is a lighthearted romp through a massive fandom that has touched on Japanese and Western audiences alike for 35 years. (</span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Build Fighters</i></span> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">is actually part of the 35</span><sup><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">th</span></sup> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">anniversary celebration, which will continue with the next series, </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Reconguista in G</i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">, </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">being created by Gundam&#8217;s original creator, Yoshiyuki “Kill Em All” Tomino</span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">. Yeah, that&#8217;s not a misspelling; Japanese audiences prefer the hard “g” sound than </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Reconquista</i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">. Figures.)</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">Set in near-future Japan, it takes the real life industry of Gunpla – plastic Gundam models – and using </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">magic (basically)</span> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">animates them inside of special playing boards, letting their weapons actually fire lasers and bullets and missiles, and they actually explode when destroyed. Nobody, though, is actually harmed, though they take it very seriously. The series itself engages in a ton of lampshading and parodying Japanese anime shows where the basis is on collecting and winning in tournaments, over just how silly it is to be </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>so super serious</i></span> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">about playing tournaments with </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>toys</i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">. </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">It does so not in a blatant way, either, although I&#8217;m not sure “subtle” would describe it either. It merely makes it all work in a spectacularly entertaining fashion.</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">Here&#8217;s the weird thing, for me: I am a 25-year old white male, one who has lost a good amount of weight in the past couple of months and doesn&#8217;t look like he lives in his mother&#8217;s basement, and here I am being very unhappy that this show is ending.</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">What kind of a person am I?</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">There&#8217;s a small bit of me that looks at the rest of me and wonders, “Jesus, man, are you ever going to grow up and get into the adult world?” I shouldn&#8217;t really care about this show. It is entirely fictional. It&#8217;s definitely a niche thing. And yet I am still rather pissed that, in all their wisdom, Sunrise is only giving it 25 episodes. That will make it the shortest </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Gundam</i></span> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">TV show by far, with the next shortest, </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>After War Gundam X</i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">, coming in at 39 episodes. (Note: I haven&#8217;t watched </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Gundam X</i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">, so I have no idea what it&#8217;s like. Other than it&#8217;s post-apocalyptic. Sorta.)</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">This show deserves, at the very least, a second season. There are so many questions left unresolved. For instance, one of the protagonists, Reiji, comes from another world (and so does the “big bad”.) We only see him disappear in a flash of light once, although his other world is talked about a bit as a subplot. But ending it </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>now</i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">? We have no idea what is going on with that! It&#8217;s barely even touched upon, only enough to make us wonder “What?” And maybe that is the point, that not everything should be explained, that there should still be some mystery in life – for else, why live?</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">To be fair, though, I don&#8217;t think the creators of this show were going anywhere that philosophical.</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">But what really makes this show so wonderful is that it is a total release. Living in the Washington DC area, in a place where a hotdog costs one and a half reverse mortgages, bombarded every day with politics and scheming and BS, </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Gundam Build Fighters</i></span> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">is my little escape valve. I can watch a brilliantly colorful world come to life and just forget about all the insanity going on in 3D land. I can cheer on the protagonists, Reiji and Sei, as they fight to win the championship. I can curse their opponents for underhanded moves. I can laugh at the zany jokes and awe at the cool moves.</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">At the end of the day, </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Gundam Build Fighters</i></span> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">is pure catharthis. In many ways, it&#8217;s therapy.</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">The weird thing especially for me is how I have come to care about the characters in a real way. I&#8217;ve always known that an author&#8217;s first job is to make the reader care about a character. This is one of the ironclad rules of writing. But for me, I never had the reactions others had. For example, some people said they cried when reading the last </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Harry Potter </i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">book. </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">I don&#8217;t think I could do that. </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Harry Potter</i></span> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">is, after all, fictional. And yet here, I am practically crying over the end of this series. I don&#8217;t want it to end. It needs to go to at least 50 episodes. That makes me feel quite sad.</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">In many ways, it reminds me of the first anime I ever watched: </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Digimon Adventure</i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">. I saw that in second grade, and I remember being quite teary eyed as a child over the ending. I wanted it to go on forever. And now I want this to go on forever. I hate goodbyes. I hate endings. I just need that release to keep going. I need </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>something</i></span> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">to keep my sanity in a world gone completely insane.</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">That&#8217;s why I love </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Gundam Build Fighters</i></span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">, and ultimately all Japanese animation. It is a total cathartic release from the world in ways that I cannot obtain as readily from Western media. Don&#8217;t ask me why or how. I don&#8217;t understand it either. Maybe it&#8217;s because Hollywood has just completely run out of ideas. I don&#8217;t know.</span>

<span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">But, for whatever reason, </span><span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;"><i>Gundam Build Fighters</i></span> <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">has helped me keep myself intact in this world by giving me a portal to another. That&#8217;s a very useful thing to have at any age.</span>

 <span style="font-family: 'Open Sans', sans-serif;">And with that, my friends, it is now time for me to go watch the final episode. It&#8217;s going to be a good one.</span>
