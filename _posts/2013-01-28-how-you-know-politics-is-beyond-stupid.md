[From some random reading on Wikipedia](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Party_switching_in_the_United_States#cite_ref-48):

> 2009 – Dale Swenson, [Kansas](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kansas "Kansas") State Representative from [Wichita](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wichita,_Kansas "Wichita, Kansas") switched January 12, 2009 moments after being sworn into office as Republican for eighth term <sup id="cite_ref-48"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Party_switching_in_the_United_States#cite_note-48">[48]</a></sup> In 2010, Swenson&#8217;s Democratic opponent in 2008, [Leslie Osterman](http://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Leslie_Osterman&action=edit&redlink=1 "Leslie Osterman (page does not exist)"), switched to Republican and defeated Swenson.<sup id="cite_ref-49"><a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Party_switching_in_the_United_States#cite_note-49">[49]</a></sup>
> 
> &nbsp;

That&#8217;s right. Candidate A switches from Republican to Democrat. Candidate B, his opponent, then switches from Democrat to Republican.

And you think the two parties are actually different.
