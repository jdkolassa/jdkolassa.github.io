I cannot stop writing. I write all the time, try to sneak some in during work, sneak some in at home; writing science fiction is like a drug. The more I do it, the more I have to. I used to procrastinate all the time&#8211;but now, procrastination is painful.

Not with regards to blog writing, though.

I&#8217;ve been wondering what the proper frequency of updates is. There are many blogs who update on a daily basis. i09, one of my favorite blogs that covers the whole science fiction/technology scene, updates around 20-30 times a day (however, it should be noted that i09 is run by Gawker, a company, and has a large number of writers.) Me, meanwhile, I just don&#8217;t have that much stuff to talk about. I do have some things in the works&#8211;I still need to post that entry about my political history, but its hard pinning down when I got these crazy ideas into my head&#8211;but what is there for me to write about? Besides, my time is better spent on my real writing.

Some other writers, though, seem to have far different opinions. I was taking a look at Charles Stross&#8217; blog, &#8220;Antipope,&#8221; and he seems to post every day&#8211;including bringing in a guest blogger to post when he&#8217;s out for a week. That&#8217;s dedication. Or maybe he had some snacks he wanted to get rid of. John Scalzi is similar, his blog has about 1-3 posts each day. Jim Butcher seems to be a bit more lax, posting roughly once a week (although I&#8217;m not entirely sure if that&#8217;s actually Jim Butcher or an intern updating the blog. In any case, his Livejournal hasn&#8217;t been updated since late 2008.)

It makes me question how often I should updating. For me, right now, it really doesn&#8217;t matter, since I have a grand total of four comments (two of which are written by myself) and very few visitors. I also don&#8217;t have much material to share, at the moment, so there isn&#8217;t as much of a reason to come visit. My original plan was to update twice a week, and have somewhere around 4-5 entries written up ahead of time. _That_ hasn&#8217;t gone so well, but fortunately, the frequency has, more or less. Last week was an oddity, but for the most part, I&#8217;ve actually been posting 3-4 times a week. About what, I&#8217;m not entirely sure. Flying deep fried bacon? Yeah, probably. I don&#8217;t remember.

Twitter is a different beast entirely. Before starting my media job, I rarely used Twitter. In fact, I still don&#8217;t really see the point of Twitter; it&#8217;s just a time waster. Nobody is really saying anything of use&#8211;snarky, maybe, but not important. _But_, no one else has seemed to caught on to this, so I am forced to join the Twitter cloud and get involved. I got a Twitter back in college, rarely used it, came here&#8230;and found TweetDeck for Chrome, which now sits in a pinned tab on the left, beyond all my actual work, and I check it repeatedly, sometimes around every 30 seconds. (Gimme a sec&#8230;ha, another joke about Charlie Sheen. K, back.) I&#8217;ve turned into a compulsive Tweep, reposting articles about what&#8217;s going on in Wisconsin and other political issues, posting about science fiction and technological development, and also posting about&#8230;I don&#8217;t know, random things. Things which are okay to have on LinkedIn (since my Twitter account and LinkedIn are connected) but don&#8217;t really have any other sort of &#8220;point.&#8221; In fact, my Twittering has become so intense I had to stop Twitter from publishing to my wall on Facebook because it was beginning to annoy my friends. (FYI: My Facebook is only open to those I have met in real life, mostly. No, you can&#8217;t get in.)

I don&#8217;t know what this means. Am I only good for writing really small blurbs now? I hope that&#8217;s not the case&#8211;I still have a novel sequence _magnum opus_ to write over the next year. Maybe it will help me write short stories, however, which I have found to be far more difficult&#8211;you think that something shorter would be easier, but it actually takes far greater skill to pack everything into a smaller box than it does a larger one. As Blaise Pascal famously said, &#8220;I would have written a shorter letter, but I did not have the time.&#8221;

How many times do you think is reasonable to update a blog?
