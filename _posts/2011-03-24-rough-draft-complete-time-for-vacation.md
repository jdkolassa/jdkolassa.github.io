So I haven&#8217;t updated in a full week, but that&#8217;s because I&#8217;ve been busy working my butt off to finish this long overdue draft of a story. I&#8217;ve been working on it for roughly six months, dealing with false starts, bad scenes, a schedule that was dominated by college, job-searching, and then a _job_, not the mention illness, an MIA muse and an internal editor from hell that all tried to keep me down, but I succeeded.

Last night, I was able to wrap it up and write THE END. And there is something incredibly exhilarating about writing those two simple three-letter words. I don&#8217;t think I could explain it in words, but perhaps by analogy: I will never know what the Rapture will feel like, but I suspect that it will feel something like this.

Of course, simply writing THE END does not make it so. This is only a rough/first draft (I never really made a distinction between a &#8220;rough draft&#8221; and a &#8220;first draft,&#8221; though some authors do). I&#8217;m going to let it sit for 24 hours and solidify, allow my brain to wander elsewhere and think of other things, but then I will back on task. It is not yet publishable; it&#8217;s good, but not publishable. Edits need to be made. The prose needs to be tightened in spots. The characterization needs to be improved; the plot reinforced; and the antagonists, well, I&#8217;m just not sure that antagonists are what I want in it. I&#8217;m debating replacing the antagonists with more human enemies&#8230;but when I did get to the end, I felt they still somehow worked. So that needs to be thought out and worked over.

But I can do that over the weekend. For now, my brain needs to rest. Rest, and rejuvenate, and recharge, and some other word that begins with the prefix &#8220;re-&#8221; but that isn&#8217;t coming to my mind right now.
