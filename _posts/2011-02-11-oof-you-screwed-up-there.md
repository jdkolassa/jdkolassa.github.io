I just love capturing mistakes. I try not to point out things which are bad grammar or spelling&#8211;that just comes out as me being a nitpicker Grammar Nazi&#8211;but sometimes, I can&#8217;t help myself.

[Like on this piece about the Ford Focus Electric](http://media.ford.com/article_display.cfm?article_id=34044), which points out what car parts you won&#8217;t have to worry about, since the car won&#8217;t have them. Point out which one shouldn&#8217;t be there. (I&#8217;ll give you a hint in the list.)

> Here are the top 25 items that usually require inspection, maintenance or replacement during the 10-year, 150,000-mile life of a conventional car that the driver of a Ford Focus Electric will never have to worry about:
> 
> Air filter
  
> Alternator
  
> <span style="text-decoration: underline;"><em><strong>Battery*!!!1</strong></em><em>†‡</em></span>
  
> Clutch
  
> Fuel filter
  
> Fuel injectors/fuel pump
  
> Motor mounts
  
> Motor oil
  
> Muffler
  
> Oil filter
  
> O2 sensors
  
> Power steering fluid
  
> Radiator
  
> Radiator hose, lower
  
> Radiator hose, upper
  
> Serpentine belt
  
> Spark plugs
  
> Spark plug wires
  
> Starter
  
> Thermostat
  
> Timing belt
  
> Transmission adjustment (automatics)
  
> Transmission filter (automatics)
  
> Transmission fluid or oil
  
> Water pump**
  
>** 

Right. An electric car would _never_ need a battery!

Good going Ford. Made yourself look real great there. Maybe next time you should think about these things.

It&#8217;d be one thing if they actually stepped forward and explained a battery&#8217;s inclusion on the list&#8211;are they saying that no one would have to service those lithium batteries? I am aware that they are different from what we put into our flashlights&#8211;but they don&#8217;t. The press release just ends. I&#8217;m assuming that they were trying to get to a nice, divisible-by-5 number that looks goods in quick figures, and someone just tossed that in there, but c&#8217;mon, they could have gone with just 24 things a Ford Focus Electric driver doesn&#8217;t have to worry about. Then they could say its Jack Bauer&#8217;s car. And who wouldn&#8217;t want to drive that?
