[Congress’s Piracy Blacklist Plan: A Cure Worse than the Disease? | Techland | TIME.com](http://techland.time.com/2011/11/07/congresss-piracy-blacklist-plan-a-cure-worse-than-the-disease/2/).

It&#8217;s a dumb law that will criminalize just casual piracy, which isn&#8217;t even really piracy. Hell, using the &#8220;ShareThis&#8221; feature on Youtube to post a music video to Facebook would get you fined and your accounts shut down. That&#8217;s just stupid.

[The fact that Facebook, Twitter, Yahoo, Google, Tumblr, and other major Internet giants are coming out against this bill should really tell you something.](http://techland.time.com/2011/11/17/sopa-wont-stop-online-piracy-would-censor-everyone-else/)

It&#8217;s a dumb law that will utterly destroy American values of free speech in order to go after a crime in a manner that doesn&#8217;t even deal with it that well. (What are you going to do about foreign websites, anyways?) It&#8217;s a wild overreaching of federal power that should have conservatives&#8211;particularly states-rights conservatives&#8211;up in arms. Naturally, they aren&#8217;t (at least not that much.)

Just say no to SOPA.

UPDATE: Here&#8217;s [the text of the bill](http://judiciary.house.gov/hearings/pdf/112%20HR%203261.pdf). In addition, [read this great summary](https://www.eff.org/deeplinks/2011/10/sopa-hollywood-finally-gets-chance-break-internet) from the Electronic Frontier Federation.

I also want to point out that I&#8217;m not a fan of intellectual piracy. Contrary to many libertarians, I actually do believe in intellectual property&#8211;but within reason. Frankly, SOPA is not within reason whatsoever. Not that that has ever stopped our Most Esteemed Leaders of the People&#8217;s Commissariat in Washington, but it should be noted.
