[Iain M. Banks, author of the Culture novels, has passed away](http://io9.com/iain-m-banks-author-of-the-culture-novels-has-passed-512173168).

> In April, Banks [posted a brave message](http://io9.com/iain-m-banks-posts-a-sad-brave-announcement-of-his-im-468426237) announcing that he was suffering from terminal cancer, and fellow author <a href="https://twitter.com/cstross/statuses/343740022924648448" target="_blank">Charles Stross has confirmed</a> that Banks died this morning. It&#8217;s difficult to express Banks&#8217; contribution to science fiction, engrossing readers in the vast and complex universe of the Culture for a quarter of a century.

I read the first novel in the Culture series, _Consider Pheblas_, last year. It was genuinely brilliant and I have been looking forward to reading the rest of his Culture novels, though I haven&#8217;t due to dealing with Darth Real Life lately.

_Consider Pheblas_ was like reading a version of _Star Wars_ with modern sensibilities. While I didn&#8217;t like all of it (the part with the degenerate wretches on the island on the doomed Orbital grossed me out and I thought it was unnecessary) it had the same sense of majesty, the wide open _vastness_ of proper space opera, and even a bit of space fantasy. Obviously the tone is quite different&#8211;there&#8217;s no Force or quasi-mystical elements, at least in that book&#8211;but it was really something that I got into and thoroughly enjoyed. The man was a fantastic writer, even outside the science fiction genre, and in terms of scifi I wish it got more traction in America and help shape science fiction here. Mainly because a lot of the scifi I see these days is very postmodern introspective junk that just bores me.

He will be sorely missed.
