I&#8217;m not doing NaNoWriMo. Again.

Perhaps it&#8217;s because I don&#8217;t have time and I&#8217;m really getting in the way of myself. Perhaps it&#8217;s because I just want to move on my own schedule. Perhaps it&#8217;s because I always feel NaNoWriMo is extremely gimmicky and gimmicky doesn&#8217;t work for me. Perhaps it&#8217;s because, at the moment, I want to get out a short story, not a novel. 

For whatever reason, I won&#8217;t do NaNoWriMo again for the umpteenth year in a row. That&#8217;s okay; I don&#8217;t have anything against people who do do it. It&#8217;s just not for me, and I suspect it&#8217;s not for a lot of people.

I have been writing, however, and more than just code. I have been trying out the program Scrivener, and while I&#8217;m a bit apprehensive of paying $40 for a piece of software that isn&#8217;t a resource-intensive first person shooter, I think I&#8217;m going to end up buying it. It&#8217;s actually a great word processor combined with a fantastic project management suite. I still want to try it, though, for the next 24 days I have it free. I think I can get at least 3,000 more words out of it on that.

Now, if only Aeon Timeline can be brought over to Windows, that would be excellent.
