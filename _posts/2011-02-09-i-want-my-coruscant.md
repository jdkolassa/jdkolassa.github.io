The Daily Mail&#8211;or, as some of my colleagues call it, the &#8220;Daily Fail&#8221;-[-published a story on cities of the future](http://www.dailymail.co.uk/sciencetech/article-1354540/Tree-towers-Taiwan-mega-pyramid-Tokyo-Cities-future-here.html), and I just have to say: wow.

That is a _lot_ of huge pictures. Do the editors get paid extra to put them up like that?

But past the snark, I do find future architecture and city planning to be fascinating. Ever since I saw that History Channel _Modern Marvels_ episode on the giant city-tower they were planning to build in Tokyo in, oh, 1 Berjillion AD, I&#8217;ve been fascinated. To think that we could, with the right technologies, radically overhaul our urban areas, and even change the fundamental living arrangements of our society, is breathtaking. For a while now, I&#8217;ve been thinking that the optimal living situation on Earth would be to have most of the planet uninhabited wilderness, with arcologies scattered across the globe, connected via either high-speed rail, air transport, or a global highway network, or maybe a combination of all three. (Who says libertarians don&#8217;t care about the environment?)

The ocean towers are particularly interesting. The oceans are really the last frontier on Earth (space being the last frontier for everything.) Why haven&#8217;t we colonized them yet? We could have _Seaquest_ and talking dolphins and a genius kid who won&#8217;t shut up and that nerdy guy and then go into outer space to fight those&#8211;whoops, went into season 3. Sorry about that. \*Ahem\* Needless to say, there is great opportunity here for better use of our oceans, and also some interesting political questions, namely: If you&#8217;re out there, in the middle of an ocean, outside of any national territory, do you become a new state? (Well, if you can hold your &#8220;territory,&#8221; that is&#8230;)

There is one thing I have to gripe about, though: that third picture, the tower with the big balloons. The caption says it will have &#8220;helium-filled observation decks&#8221; moving up its side, and work will apparently start next year. To which I say: yeah right. Does anybody seriously think this will actually come to pass within the next, oh, let&#8217;s be generous, 50 years? I like interesting ideas and glimpses to the future, but let&#8217;s be honest with ourselves here, that isn&#8217;t going to be finished until I&#8217;m a pensioner. At least the Shimizu Mega-City Pyramid says its still only a &#8220;pipe dream.&#8221; That thing is just&#8230;nuts.

I should point out that I don&#8217;t actually want Coruscant, the famous city planet capital of that galaxy far, far away. It might be a cool place to visit a few times, maybe even bearable to do work there, but who in their right minds would want to visit a planet entirely covered by mile-tall skyscrapers? You&#8217;d be dead inside. No, I&#8217;d rather live out in the countryside, where I feel the dirt under my feet if I choose, go camping in the woods, fish in the streams, and ride giant grizzly bears.

You heard me.
