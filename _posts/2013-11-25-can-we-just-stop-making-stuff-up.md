The title of a recent article at _The New Republic_ reads:

> The Period Is Pissed
  
> When did our plainest punctuation mark become so aggressive?

[The author then goes on to assume that adding a period at the end of your texts indicates you&#8217;re angry.](http://www.newrepublic.com/article/115726/period-our-simplest-punctuation-mark-has-become-sign-anger)

This is one of the plainest cases of someone just making shit up. Ok, so he cites a professor of linguistics and the editor of something called the Awl (presumably short for &#8220;The Awful,&#8221; judging by the editor&#8217;s comments.) But this guy is definitely smoking something.

Look, a period at the end of sentences is not indicating aggressiveness. It is _proper grammar_. Nothing more, nothing less. Yes, some people don&#8217;t do that and use line breaks because it&#8217;s more efficient to type that way on mobile phones. Not technically proper, but it&#8217;s a convenience to use such a wonderful device. I have no problem with that. But if you start assuming that using periods in a message indicates &#8220;anger,&#8221; well, then you&#8217;re going to have a _lot_ of problems in your life. You&#8217;re probably going to end up hating a lot of people or feeling really bad about yourself. And then your life is going to get worse.

Do yourself a favor. Don&#8217;t assume. It makes an ass out of you and me.

EDIT: I am now very angry at my high school for making me think grammar is spelled with an &#8220;e&#8221;.
