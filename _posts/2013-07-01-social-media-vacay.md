Everyone tells me I need to be on social media if I want my stuff promoted. I gotta be on there to be heard, to be seen, to get things done.

If I&#8217;ve learned anything in the past few years, though, it&#8217;s that that may not be necessarily true. Indeed, social media has some serious pitfalls. My favorite ones are flamewars with trolls. It was one of them, in fact, that led me to this policy of taking a social media vacation.

I&#8217;ve learned that I cannot resist getting into arguments; I can&#8217;t resist trolls. That something I lost, somewhere, aand I need to reclaim that. How? I don&#8217;t know. But a prerequisite has to be stepping back from social media. It&#8217;s hurting my productivity, hurting my wider social sphere, and ultimately it&#8217;s hurting my brain.

So I&#8217;m taking a break from Twitter & Facebook. I&#8217;ve downloaded an app that only does Twitter DM&#8217;s, and I&#8217;ll keep using Facebook Messenger so people can get in touch with me. Other than that, just Gmail & this blog. Thanks to the magic of WordPress, I can still write status updates. And thanks to the magic of RSS, I can autopost these to Twitter and Facebook.

I see this as an extension of some cutting I&#8217;ve already done in my life. A few months ago I uninstalled Steam and basically eliminated computer games from my life. Now I need to continue the process and get rid of&#8211;at least temporarily&#8211;another serious distraction.

I&#8217;m not sure why I had to blog this, really, other than to tell my friends who are going to start wondering, but if you stumble across this and read this, that&#8217;s what this is about. I will still be politically minded; I will still have strong opinions about political philosophy and government. I will still write, though hopefully not on Twitter (I will maintain my vow, I will maintain my vow&#8230;) but through other channels.

That&#8217;s all I have. If you know me personally, you can still hit me up through messaging. If you don&#8217;t, you can always comment here.
