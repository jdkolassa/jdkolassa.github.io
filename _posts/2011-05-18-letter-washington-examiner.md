I wrote a letter to the editor of the Washington Examiner, responding to this letter on the paper &#8220;[distorting facts about federal spending](http://washingtonexaminer.com/opinion/letters-editor/2011/05/letters-editor-may-17-2011).&#8221;

_Riiiiigggghhhht._

My response:

> If you must carp about the Examiner&#8217;s editorial board, how about at least keeping the facts straight?
> 
> Your May 17 letter did nothing to enhance your credibility or reputation, particularly: &#8220;Under President Bush, the national debt grew from the surplus he inherited to $8.2 trillion in 2006.&#8221;
> 
> Actually, in 2001, national debt was at $5.8 trillion. What you&#8217;re conflating is a budget surplus&#8211;which is year to year&#8211;with the overall national debt, which lasts forever. I&#8217;m not defending Bush&#8211;you were spot on about the wars&#8211;but if you&#8217;re going to criticize him (and by extension, the Examiner) at least do it properly.

I think it may not have been printed because I seem to be sucking up to the paper. But I think that if anyone criticizes another, they should at least get their facts straight, and that fellow clearly doesn&#8217;t. I&#8217;ll accept criticism of myself if its based on facts and reason (or, if of my fiction, is not completely unreasonable and &#8220;out there&#8221;), but not if its based on misunderstandings and fictions of its own.
