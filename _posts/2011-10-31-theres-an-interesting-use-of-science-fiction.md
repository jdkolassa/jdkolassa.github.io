[Deciphering eurozone: financial stability quest a study in surrealism | The Japan Times Online](http://www.japantimes.co.jp/text/nb20111031jp.html).

Mostly because I want to point out that what&#8217;s going on in Europe is just economic suicide, and, well, this guy is pretty much right on the money. But I also never thought to call it _this_:

> <span style="font-family: Geneva, Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif; font-size: 16px; line-height: 20px;">EFSF stands for European Financial Stability Facility. Or so they say. I can only see it as standing for European Financial Science Fiction. How can it be anything else given the nature of the arrangement?</span>

Huh. Good point. Now I;m wondering if there are any examples of &#8220;economic fiction&#8221; out there (I think there are, but they&#8217;re not coming to me. Other than _Atlas Shrugged_.) I don&#8217;t think I&#8217;ll be reading that Stephen King story, though. That just weirds me out way too much.
