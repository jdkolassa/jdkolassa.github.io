[Anti-Smoking Crusaders Add MLB to Hit List &#8211; PRNewser](http://www.mediabistro.com/prnewser/anti-smoking-crusaders-add-mlb-to-hit-list_b16809).

_Seriously_?

Clicking through to the _Wall Street Journal_ story, [the scene in question is:](http://blogs.wsj.com/health/2011/03/07/rango-smokes-the-box-office-and-for-some-thats-a-problem/?mod=google_news_blog)

> <span style="font-family: Arial, Helvetica, sans-serif;">where Rango (the chameleon hero of the film)  swallows a cigar being smoked by another character (Bad Bill, a bullying gila monster) and then breathes/burps fire into Bill’s face.</span>

Really? They want to go crazy over this movie for _this_?

As if tobacco is even a big thing anymore. Sure, some of my colleagues smoke, but not many, and most people are more interested in weed.

Smoking is not a controversial thing anymore, for any age group. Neither is drinking. (The _Journal_ just ran an [interesting article on parents allowing their underage kids drink at home](http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424052748703386704576186380879004132.html), a plan I certainly approve of. Not that my parents let me drink, but when I had some communion wine when I was 10&#8211;whoo boy! Didn&#8217;t drink for almost ten years! Several of my friends have been drinking since they were 12.) Hell, neither is sex or cussing. How many boys by the time they&#8217;re sixteen have either lost their virginity or seen a considerable amount of porn through the interwebs? Let&#8217;s face it, a _lot_.

The organizations moving against this, I feel, are relics of a bygone age, an age that no longer exists. (Indeed, I would doubt if it _ever_ existed.) How very appropriate that one of them is called &#8220;Legacy,&#8221; but what legacy are they trying to promote? The social conservative legacy? That&#8217;s what it sounds like to me, conserving a society that no longer exists.

Hey, Cheryl, here&#8217;s a concept&#8211;why don&#8217;t you stop babying kids and adapt to a new era when they&#8217;re already more independent? I think if one grants teenagers more independence _along with_ more responsibility, they will fly to it like girls to a _Twilight_ showing, and we&#8217;ll have both teenagers _and_ future adults be better off. But that can&#8217;t happen unless people like Cheryl stop screaming and whining every time the image of a cigarette or a beer appear in a movie. That can&#8217;t happen unless we accept that these things exist and that ignoring them won&#8217;t go away.

Too bad ignoring groups like Legacy won&#8217;t make them go away.
