So over a week ago, a large number of people went marching in New York City with the hashtag #climatemarch. The idea was to raise awareness of the dangers of global warming &#8211; excuse me, _climate change_ &#8211; and get people, specifically politicians, to act on it. How? To be honest I&#8217;m not sure of the specifics, but it always seems to come back to higher taxes, more government control over the economy, and doing away with capitalism.

Nevermind that capitalism is why they even had a New York City to have the climate change march in.

I want to lay out a few disclaimers before I continue, to outline my views on climate change. These will take the form of a Q&A.

  1. **Is climate change happening?
  
** Yeah. I mean, everything changes, so it would be silly to say that the climate doesn&#8217;t change. Nothing is truly static except death and taxes, and we may be getting close to getting rid of one of those.
  2. **Is it anything to be worried about?**
  
    Not really. Sure, there will be things here and there that climate change will affect that we&#8217;ll have to adapt to, and it may lead to some minor detrimental affects in some parts of the world, but overall it&#8217;s not something to get your panties in a twist over. It isn&#8217;t going to lead us to extinction, it&#8217;s not going to be the end of the world. Humanity has adapted for several thousand years, and I think it will adapt for several thousand more (unless superintelligent AI get us.)
  3. **Are humans the primary cause of climate change?**
  
    I don&#8217;t find this plausible. I can accept that we&#8217;re a tertiary or even secondary cause of climate change, because we definitely have an affect on the environment. But even if the planet is warming, can we really blame it on us? What other natural processes are out there? Have you ever looked up and saw that giant ball of exploding gas in the sky and wondered, &#8220;Hmm, could the sun have anything to do with it?&#8221; Earth is far larger, far more resilient, and far more complex than I think most people give it credit for.
  4. **Should the government do anything about climate change?**
  
    Absolutely not! This is the government, mind you, that screwed up the Middle East, spent over $600 million on a _website_, can&#8217;t keep track of billions of dollars of its budget, regularly violates civil liberties around the world, and is deeply in bed with large corporations in a cronyist scam that has gone back for decades. The last thing anybody should want is giving this incompetent bloated organization any more power than it already has &#8211; _especially_ over such important things as the energy and the environment.

There, now that I have that off my chest&#8230;

How much cleaner would the world be if all those hundreds of thousands of people, instead of taking this trip, just stayed home? Oh, yes, they care about the environment. They care about it so much they helped pollute it even more.

And then there are the images of the trash they left behind. How many Starbucks cups did they throw out? How much plastic did they use? It&#8217;s disgusting. Care about the environment, do they? Yeah right.

## The Absurdity

One of the big reasons I personally can&#8217;t take climate change and the #climatemarch seriously is how it&#8217;s being blamed as the cause of everything. Open up any newspaper and you will see an article saying that &#8220;climate change&#8221; is the reason for <a href="http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/monkey-cage/wp/2014/07/03/a-hotter-drier-middle-east-climate-could-threaten-stability/" target="_blank">the political instability in the Middle East</a>. (Subtitle: &#8220;Why are deserts hot?&#8221;) Hurricane Sandy and local weather patterns have all been blamed on climate change and global warming. <a href="http://wattsupwiththat.com/2014/05/09/claim-climate-change-will-kill-the-bees/" target="_blank">The bees are being blamed on it too</a>, even though it strikes me as yet another media panic. Even the European Space Agency is <a href="http://www.slate.com/blogs/future_tense/2014/09/29/antarctic_ice_melt_causes_small_shift_in_gravity.html" target="_blank">now saying that global warming is affecting</a> Earth&#8217;s gravitational field.

I can&#8217;t even.

And now the _New Scientist_ magazine <a href="http://www.newscientist.com/article/dn26243-world-on-track-for-worstcase-warming-scenario.html#.VCxnFBbTdmZ" target="_blank">is saying that we&#8217;re on track</a> for the &#8220;worst case&#8221; scenario. One Tweeter made the case that we&#8217;re headed towards extinction because of this. The problem with the article and the evidence, in my view, is that it isn&#8217;t really based on empirical data. It&#8217;s based on models:

> <span style="color: #000000;">The bleak image is brought home when emissions over the last few decades are plotted against projections for the future. Models predict how much the world will warm depending on how much we emit in future. Scientists typically look at four different possible futures, ranging from an uber-green society to a worst-case scenario, in which no action is taken to combat global warming. Le Quéré and her colleagues show how today&#8217;s emissions are near-perfectly in line with the worst-case scenario. This means that, according to scientists&#8217; best estimates, the world will be as much as 5.4 °C warmer in 2100 than it was before the industrial revolution.</span>

The chart in the article begins in 1980 and ends in 2100. Here&#8217;s a massive problem with this:

### _There&#8217;s no way you can predict what the world will be like in 2100!_

Look at predictions from 90 years ago. How many were somewhat on track, and how far off were the rest? Could anyone have predicted the rise of the Internet, of Twitter, of the smartphone and now the &#8220;smart house,&#8221; of Dropbox and Spotify and global air travel and drone cargo ships and the private attempts to colonize Mars? In some places they may have gotten the basic gist of things &#8211; although, even in the case of Mars, they screwed up badly &#8211; but in the vast majority of cases they hadn&#8217;t a clue what the world would be like in a century.

That&#8217;s the same thing here. You can point to your models, but they&#8217;re just that: models. You can play around with models all day long, change them, tweak them, etc. German reporter and podcaster Fabian Scherschel made this point <a href="http://sixgun.org/episodes/lo347" target="_blank">when he brought up his skepticism of climate change alarmism on his podcast</a>, _Linux Outlaws_ (which, as you can imagine, doesn&#8217;t usually wade into this subject.) The data we have really only goes back two hundred years or so, not long enough to judge how the climate is being affected, which has been going on here for _millions and millions of years_.

And yet #climatemarch activists want to parade around and demand we end capitalism and the modern world because some model someone dreamt up said that we&#8217;re all going to die. Well, I have a model that we&#8217;re all going to die, but I can save us &#8211; if you just give me $5,000. Will you march for me then?

Oh, and by the way: <a href="http://wattsupwiththat.com/2014/06/06/antarctic-sea-ice-increase-and-global-warming/" target="_blank">sea ice has been increasing lately</a>.

This isn&#8217;t activism. This is pure fearmongering.

## The Alternative to #climatemarch

Ronald Bailey of _reason_ has done a much better job than I could ever do knocking these guys off their pedestal. See his story on #climatemarch <a href="http://reason.com/archives/2014/09/22/the-peoples-climate-march" target="_blank">here</a> and his story on #FloodWallStreet <a href="http://reason.com/archives/2014/09/23/flood-wall-street-climate-change-protest" target="_blank">here</a>. He takes aim at their assertions and blows holes in them rather efficiently. They don&#8217;t even know the best strategies to address their own problem. The whole thing is just ridiculous.

What&#8217;s the alternative? I&#8217;m honestly not sure. I think, for starters, we can continue to utilize capitalism to experiment with new technologies in the energy sector, as well as <a href="http://www.ox.ac.uk/media/news_stories/2011/labgrown_meat_would.html" target="_blank">lab-grown meat that would cut emissions by 96%</a>. There are plenty of free market environmental alternatives out there that could be explored, but one thing that is important is having private property rights. Nothing halts environmental degradation better than by [avoiding the tragedy of the commons](http://www.econlib.org/library/Enc/TragedyoftheCommons.html). But I don&#8217;t really know what will fix the environment, if it truly needs saving. But you know what? That&#8217;s okay. The beauty of being a capitalist is that you don&#8217;t have to know everything; you just have to know that you have a system that is designed to discover and find things out, that champions experimentation and innovation.

That&#8217;s the alternative. It doesn&#8217;t rely on fearmongering. It isn&#8217;t based in hypocrisy. It&#8217;s not absurd. And it&#8217;s not insane. It&#8217;s just dealing with human beings as individuals. You don&#8217;t need to march for that. You don&#8217;t have to do anything special. All you have to do is respect other people as individuals, and live your life.
